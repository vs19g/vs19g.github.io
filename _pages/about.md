I am a Physics PhD Candidate at **Florida State University**, working at the **John D. Fox Superconducting Linear Accelerator Laboratory**. My research sits at the intersection of nuclear structure, astrophysics, and advanced instrumentation.

### Research & Expertise
My primary work involves studying $(\alpha, p)$ reactions critical to understanding X-ray bursts and the hot CNO cycle. As an experimentalist, I specialize in:
* **Detector Development:** Designing and fabricating custom gas-filled proportional counters and silicon strip arrays.
* **Digital DAQ:** Upgrading and integrating CAEN-based data acquisition systems (1740/1725) using FSUDAQ and CoMPASS.
* **Simulations:** Utilizing ROOT, Garfield++, and COMSOL to model detector physics and electric fields.

### Background
Before coming to FSU, I was a Junior Research Fellow at the **Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR)** in Mumbai, where I worked on digital signal processing for the **INGA array** and characterized $LaBr_3(Ce)$ detectors. I hold a dual degree from **BITS Pilani - Hyderabad** in Physics and Chemical Engineering, where my thesis focused on nanophotonics-enabled water desalination.

### Teaching & Mentorship
I am passionate about physics education, having served as a Teaching Assistant for College Physics (PHY 2053/2054) and currently serve as the **Tandem Maintenance Coordinator** at the Fox Lab, where I train incoming graduate students on laboratory safety and accelerator operations.

### Beyond the Lab
When I’m not at the beamline, I am an avid long-distance runner. I recently achieved a 5K personal record of 21:35 and enjoy incorporating swimming into my active recovery routine. I also have a keen interest in geographical topography and the intersection of physics and artistic design.
